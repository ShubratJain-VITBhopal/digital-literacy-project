# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do This |
|---|---------|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and bio — future employers and internship coordinators will look you up. |
| 2 | **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or a future employer saw this?" If the answer is no, don't post it. |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings.** Regularly review and update the privacy settings on all platforms to control who can see your personal content. |
| 4 | **Give credit where it's due.** Always attribute quotes, images, and ideas to their original creators when sharing someone else's work. |
| 5 | **Report harmful content.** If you witness cyberbullying, harassment, or misinformation, use the platform's reporting tools and, if necessary, contact authorities. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Avoid This |
|---|------------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal information publicly.** Avoid posting your phone number, address, college ID, bank details, or daily routine on public profiles. |
| 2 | **Don't post in anger.** Emotional posts about classmates, teachers, or institutions can damage relationships and your reputation permanently — the internet never forgets. |
| 3 | **Don't engage with or spread misinformation.** Always verify news from reliable sources before sharing. Forwarding false content — even unintentionally — is harmful. |
| 4 | **Don't accept friend/follow requests from unknown people.** Strangers online can have harmful intentions — protect your personal space and data. |
| 5 | **Don't use social media as a substitute for official communication.** For academic matters (leave requests, complaints, queries), always use official channels like college email or the VITyarthi portal. |
