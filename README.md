## What This Is

This is my submission for the CSE0001 Digital Literacy project. The brief is built around a scenario where I have been selected as a Student Digital Ambassador — someone who is supposed to help batchmates navigate the digital world more responsibly. There are five tasks, each tied to a module of the course, and all of it lives in this repository.

---

## Module Summaries

### Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic _(Module 1)_

Made a one-page infographic on Canva covering what digital literacy actually means, safe internet habits, and why having a professional online presence matters. The hard part was fitting it all on one page — ended up cutting a lot to keep it focused and readable.
📁 `task-1-presentation/`

---

### Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio _(Module 2)_

Set up profiles on **GitHub**, **LinkedIn**, and **Kaggle**. None of them are impressive at this stage, which is kind of the point — the idea is to start the record early and build on it over four years. GitHub README has the basics: name, branch, year, and what I am hoping to learn.
📁 `task-2-portfolio/`

---

### Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms _(Module 3)_

Completed the 'Solve Me First' beginner challenge on **HackerRank** — mostly to get familiar with how the platform works before placement tests make it matter. Also built a five-question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz on **Google Forms**, linked to a Google Sheet for responses.

🔗 **Google Form Quiz Link:** (https://forms.gle/bic8WoppfP5Mt9u26)
📁 `task-3-platforms/`

---

### Task 4 – Email Etiquette _(Module 4)_

Wrote two professional emails — one requesting a deadline extension from a professor, one reaching out to an internship coordinator. Also put together a Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist. The emails were trickier than expected, especially the internship one where you have to sound confident without overselling yourself as a first year with no experience yet.
📁 `task-4-email-etiquette/`

---

### Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness _(Module 5)_

Researched **UPI and online payment fraud** and wrote a case study on how a typical scam plays out step by step. Also made a prevention checklist with eight specific tips, two of which are UPI-specific. Reporting channels included: **cybercrime.gov.in** and helpline **1930**.
📁 `task-5-cybercrime/`
